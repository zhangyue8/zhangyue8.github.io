A Little Fun about Chinese Characters

I have a lot of friends who are interested in Chinese characters, but their Chinese learning is not persistent. Because they think Chinese characters are too difficult!

But in fact, there are skills to learning Chinese characters. As is known to all, Chinese characters are a kind of pictograph evolved from graphics, although after many years, the structure of Chinese characters continue to simplify, but we can still find from the body of Chinese characters as a graphic traces.

So my first advice is to remember Chinese characters as little emojis!

P1. The evolution of Chinese character "人 - people" (left to right)
You can see that the Chinese character stands on two legs. How real it is!

The second interesting Chinese character rule is the Radical.
The radicals also evolved from independent Chinese characters, Chinese characters carrying the same radical will have a similar meaning.

For example:
花 and 草 both have similar top structures: " 艹 ".
" 艹 " represents "grass/plant".
花 => "flower". 草 => "grass".
